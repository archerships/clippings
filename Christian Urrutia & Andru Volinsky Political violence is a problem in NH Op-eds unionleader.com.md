# Christian Urrutia & Andru Volinsky: Political violence is a problem in NH | Op-eds | unionleader.com
[Christian Urrutia & Andru Volinsky: Political violence is a problem in NH | Op-eds | unionleader.com](https://archive.is/2jkXF) 

 America has a political violence problem. It is growing and it has come to New Hampshire. Are our political leaders ready to respond?

The trend lines point in a grim direction.

Over the past few years, political violence has escalated in America. There was a plot to kidnap Michigan Governor Gretchen Whitmer, multiple assassination attempts against President Trump, the assassination of Charlie Kirk, Trump’s calls for the execution of our member of Congress Maggie Goodlander, the killing of Minnesota state Rep. Melissa Hortman. The list goes on and on.

These incidents are the indelible markings of a democracy in crisis. Too many of our political leaders refuse to confront this reality.

This epidemic has reached New Hampshire and our Governor has failed to meet the moment.

The Libertarian Party of New Hampshire, an organization closely associated with leading Republican political figures in our state government, threatened both authors of this piece.

The Libertarian Party of New Hampshire told its more than 100,000 followers on X that it is “perfectly permissible to kill” former Executive Councilor Andru Volinsky for advocating an income tax. The same account warned congressional candidate Christian Urrutia to “leave New Hampshire while it’s safe for you to do so” after he condemned the calls for violence against Volinsky.

Let us call these statements what they are: attempts to intimidate, to incite, and to normalize violence in our political life.

One of us, Christian Urrutia, has seen firsthand the brutal consequences of political violence. As a young lawyer, he represented the family of Victor Jara, the Chilean artist and activist who was tortured and murdered after the military coup led by Augusto Pinochet. Jara was shot more than 40 times for the simple act of opposing a dictatorship.

More than 40 years after Jara’s killing, Christian and his colleagues helped bring the principal perpetrator to justice after he fled to the United States in an effort to escape accountability.

The lesson is enduring. We must meet political violence with political accountability. That is why this moment demands clarity and courage from our leaders.

Yet Governor Kelly Ayotte has refused to confront this dangerous rhetoric. Despite being made aware of these statements, she has not condemned the words of the Libertarian Party of New Hampshire.

Governor Kelly Ayotte has previously condemned violence when it suits the moment. But when factions within Governor Ayotte’s own political orbit fan the dark flames of political violence, her voice has been absent.

Leadership is not selective. The rules cannot depend on who is targeted or who is speaking.

Political violence must have no home in our politics. Not in Washington. Not in New Hampshire.

If we are serious about protecting our democracy, then every leader must be willing to say so clearly and without hesitation.

Governor Ayotte’s silence is unacceptable and dangerous. Those who aspire to leadership should lead or step aside.
